Case Study: The Late Bronze Age Collapse (c. 1200–1177 BCE)
Confidence Level: B (Sourced to Eric Cline’s 2014/2024 framework)

Stream: Stream Three (Civilizational Foundation)

Date: May 8, 2026

1. Dating Methodology and Phasing
Onset (1250–1200 BCE): The collapse is not a single event but a "concatenation of events" beginning with a "century of drought" and seismic activity (earthquake storms).

The Terminal Signature (1177 BCE): Dated to the 8th year of Ramses III, marking the decisive battle against the Sea Peoples at the Nile Delta.

Phasing:

Complexity Peak (c. 1300 BCE): High globalization and "palace economy" interdependence.

Stress Concatenation (1225–1177 BCE): Simultaneous drought, famine, and trade disruption.

Nonlinear Simplification (1177–1130 BCE): Rapid abandonment of urban centers (Hattusa, Ugarit, Mycenae).

2. Quantifiable Variables
Trade Volume (Energy Throughput): The collapse of the tin trade (sourced as far away as Afghanistan) served as the "equivalent of an oil embargo" for the bronze-dependent civilizations.

Elite Expenditure: Archaeology shows a shift from monumental palatial building to defensive fortification immediately preceding the collapse.

Communication Tempo: The cessation of the "Amarna-style" diplomatic correspondence between Great Kings marks the functional end of the international system.

3. Literary-Cultural Foundation
The Ugarit Letters: Tablet RS 20.212 provides a real-time record of the "Reality Latency" gap: "The ships of the enemy have come here... he has done evil things to the country".

Egyptian Inscriptions: The Medinet Habu inscriptions record the psychological impact of a world falling apart: "No land could stand before their arms... they were coming forward toward Egypt".

4. Cross-Cutting Variable: Rising vs. Declining
The LBA case illustrates that complexity is a liability under stress.

The "Rising" (Highly Specialized) Failures: Ugarit and the Hittite Empire were at their peak of specialized complexity and interdependence; they collapsed completely.

The "Declining" (Decentralized) Survivor: Egypt was already in a period of relative decline (New Kingdom stagnation) and possessed a more decentralized agricultural base. It was "wounded but survived" the shocks that destroyed its more "efficient" neighbors.
