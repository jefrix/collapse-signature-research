## New Items from DeepSeek Work

### 1. MNBE as Formal Subtype

**Agenda decision:** Determine whether Multi-National Bureaucratic Empire (MNBE) should be adopted as a formal subtype in the v3.2 system taxonomy.

**Required synthesis question:** If MNBE is adopted, what is its falsifiable prediction about terminal speed relative to other agrarian-bureaucratic empire variants?

**Decision needed:** Adopt / reject / provisional subtype pending additional cases.

### 2. Narrative Retraction Capacity (NRC) Variable

**Agenda decision:** Resolve the NRC variable structure.

**Specific issues:**

- Whether mobilisability should be treated as a qualifier, subscore, or gating condition.
- Whether NRC should be dynamically scored across phases rather than assigned as a static case-level value.

**Decision needed:** Static score / dynamic score / hybrid model.

### 3. Soviet Two-Wave Self-Indictment Structure

**Agenda decision:** Determine whether state-sanctioned self-indictment accelerates collapse differently from non-state elite self-indictment.

**Key synthesis question:** Does the Soviet pattern show a two-wave structure in which controlled or sanctioned critique opens legitimacy space that later becomes uncontrollable?

**Contemporary bearing:** Directly relevant to the Anthropic case. If Anthropic's disclosure is a controlled institutional self-indictment, the Soviet comparison may help distinguish stabilizing disclosure from accelerant disclosure.

### 4. Qing Asymmetry Mechanism

**Agenda decision:** Evaluate non-state self-indictment with state silence as a distinct pattern.

**Key synthesis question:** Does elite or literary self-indictment become more destabilizing when the formal state remains silent, evasive, or narratively frozen?

**Contemporary bearing:** Assess whether any current AI/finance principals show an analogous split between non-state critique and official institutional silence.

### 5. Inca Uncodability

**Agenda decision:** Add a scope-limitation note to the self-indictment variable.

**Methodology note:** The self-indictment variable requires a contemporary written or otherwise recoverable elite discourse corpus. Cases without such a corpus should not be scored as negative by default; they should be coded as uncodable / corpus-limited.

**Decision needed:** Add explicit `uncodable_due_to_corpus_absence` status to the self-indictment methodology.

## Synthesis-Level Takeaway

The major methodological issue for May 22 is no longer simply whether self-indictment exists. It is whether different institutional positions of self-indictment — state-sanctioned, non-state with state silence, elite literary, corporate safety disclosure, or uncodable due to corpus absence — produce different collapse-relevant meanings. The synthesis should therefore avoid a single undifferentiated self-indictment variable and instead decide whether v3.2 needs subtypes, timing rules, and corpus-availability flags.
