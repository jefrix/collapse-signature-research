# “Spring Prospect” (春望) – Du Fu

- **Title:** “Spring Prospect” (春望)
- **Author:** Du Fu (杜甫, 712–770)
- **Date:** c. 757 CE
- **Type:** poetry
- **Source location:** *Quan Tang Shi* (Complete Tang Poems), juan 224. Multiple English translations: Stephen Owen, *The Poetry of Du Fu* (2016); David Hinton, *The Selected Poems of Du Fu* (1989). Chinese text available via Chinese Text Project (ctext.org).
- **Load‑bearing passage:** “國破山河在，城春草木深” – *The state is broken, but the hills and rivers remain; / In spring, the city is choked with weeds and brambles.*
- **Analytical use:** Compresses the experience of political collapse into a single image. The enduring physical landscape (“hills and rivers remain”) contrasts with the destroyed political order, registering that collapse is a human and institutional phenomenon, not a natural one. This is structural because prose arguments about institutional failure tend toward abstraction; Du Fu provides a sensorily concrete image of what administrative collapse looks like on the ground. Also cross‑culturally resonant: the Ugarit letters, the late Roman historians, and the late Habsburg literature all produce variations on “nature endures while the polity does not,” making this poem a candidate cross‑case motif.
