# Jiang Predictive History Lecture Series

- **Title:** Predictive History (YouTube lecture series)
- **Author:** Professor Jiang (channel: Predictive History)
- **Date:** Ongoing (2020s)
- **Type:** essay/lecture-series
- **Source location:** YouTube, `https://www.youtube.com/@PredictiveHistory` (access date: 2026‑05‑18). Individual lecture transcripts available via Gemini/NotebookLM for project use.
- **Load‑bearing passage:** Jiang’s core methodological insight – that nations do not merely have interests but *stories about themselves*, and that those stories shape what their political classes find imaginable – underpins the entire Stream Three literary‑strategic method.
- **Analytical use:** This is not a single quotable line but a **structural framework** for reading texts as evidence. It bridges the materialist‑quantitative analysis of collapse with the literary‑strategic module. Without this framework, the literary corpus of a late‑period civilization appears as mere cultural decoration; with it, the corpus becomes a primary source for what the system’s participants believed was possible. The project’s method memo explicitly credits Jiang.
- **Project context:** Foundational; cited in the project onboarding document, the methodology memo, and multiple Stream Three case briefs (Late Tang, Ottoman, Habsburg). Also applicable to Stream Two technocratic‑class mapping (reading their self‑narratives as Jiang would read a civilization’s literary corpus).
- **Catalogue notes:** Jiang’s method is a synthesis of older strategic‑thought traditions (Kennan’s Long Telegram, Kissinger’s *A World Restored*, Freedman’s *Strategy: A History*). When citing, credit both Jiang for the YouTube‑era formulation and the earlier tradition. Not a single reference but a framework; included here so that its methodological weight is documented alongside the more conventional literary references.
