## Methodology Notes

- **Definition of "first self-indictment signal"**: Public literary, philosophical, or elite intellectual work that names structural failure as inherent rather than contingent, produced while the regime is still operational.
- **Terminal sequence onset**: Generally defined as the point where systemic disintegration becomes irreversible (e.g., military collapse, loss of core territory, or formal abdication).

## Habsburg Anchor Case (Detailed)

**Case:** Habsburg Monarchy
**System Type:** Agrarian-bureaucratic empire (dual-sovereignty variant)

**First Self-Indictment Signal**
- Karl Kraus, *The Last Days of Mankind* (serialized 1915–1919, major portions 1916–1917)
- Kafka’s bureaucratic fiction (*The Trial*, *The Castle* written in this period)
- These works register elite self-indictment *during* the terminal sequence, not merely in retrospect.

**Onset of Terminal Sequence**
- 1918 (military collapse, dissolution of the empire)

**Lead Time**
- Approximately 1–2 years between major self-indictment works and formal collapse.

**Interpretation**
This case supports the hypothesis that high literary self-awareness can produce detectable self-indictment *before* final collapse. The signal appears while the regime still maintains administrative density and the Habsburg myth of continuity.
