## 1. STATE INVESTMENT VEHICLES: MAJOR FUNDS

### 1.1 National Integrated Circuit Industry Investment Fund ("Big Fund")

| Item | Detail | Confidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Big Fund III (Phase III) | Registered May 2024. Capital target CNY 344 billion (~USD 47 billion). The fund invests across the semiconductor chain, including AI chips, EDA tools, and advanced packaging. | A | Official filings with China's State Administration for Market Regulation; reported by *Caixin*, Reuters. |
| AI-relevant portfolio | Direct or indirect stakes in GPU-adjacent firms: Biren Technology, Moore Threads, Enflame, Cambricon. The fund also backs chip fabrication (SMIC) and design firms critical to AI compute. | B | Specific allocations are not always publicly disaggregated. |
| Governance | Ministry of Finance (the largest LP), China Development Bank Capital, other state entities. Policy direction flows from the Central Leading Group for Science and Technology. | A | The Big Fund is a classic vertical industrial-policy vehicle. |

**Analytical note:** The Big Fund is explicitly designed to circumvent US export controls on advanced chips. Its scale (Phase III alone is larger than the US CHIPS Act's manufacturing grants) and its policy direction make it a direct Chinese response to the chip-collateralized debt dynamic being mapped in Stream One. The fund's capacity to absorb losses that would bankrupt a commercial lender is effectively unlimited, making the Chinese AI hardware buildout financially distinct from the American circular-investment web.

## 3. BANK AND STATE-OWNED ENTERPRISE EXPOSURE

| Item | Detail | Confidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| ICBC, China Construction Bank, Agricultural Bank of China | All have announced "strategic support" for AI and tech infrastructure lending. Specific AI-lab credit lines are not publicly disclosed. | C | General policy signal; actual drawdowns unknown. |
| China Development Bank | Reported to have provided credit lines to chip firms (SMIC, Hua Hong) and data-center construction projects. AI infrastructure is a natural beneficiary. | C | CDB does not disclose project-level lending. |
| Ant Group (fintech) | Active investor in Zhipu and other AI startups; provides a state-linked financial channel. | B | Ant's AI investments are disclosed in capital-market filings. |

**Analytical note:** The opacity of state-bank AI lending is the most significant vehicle-layer gap in the Chinese ecosystem. The US has a known AI circular-investment web (hyperscaler equity + cloud commitments). China has a parallel structure that probably involves substantial state-bank lending, but the amounts, terms, and collateral are largely invisible. This is precisely the kind of opacity the Oracle case validation flagged.
