### Strategic Posture
The Open-Weights Hegemon. Zuckerberg is using large-scale infrastructure capex to establish a defensive position through open-weights distribution. Meta is pivoting from a legacy attention/advertising platform toward becoming a major sovereign AI compute and robotics infrastructure player. By releasing the Llama series under open-weights terms, Meta aims to fragment closed frontier model dominance while retaining significant control over the underlying standards and developer ecosystem.

### Key Claims & Positions (2025–2026)
- **Insatiable Compute Demand**: Meta raised its 2026 capital expenditure guidance to a range of $125–145 billion, nearly doubling 2025 levels ($72.2B) to secure physical-layer compute capacity.
- **Headcount vs. Compute Trade-off**: In May 2026, Zuckerberg stated that hardware infrastructure requirements are now the primary driver of organizational decisions, noting that compute costs are "crowding out headcount." This was accompanied by the elimination of approximately 8,000 corporate roles.
- **Llama as Strategic Moat**: Frames the open-weights Llama architecture as a structural counter to closed-source consolidation, positioning it as serving broader ecosystem interests while creating network effects and dependencies around Meta-controlled model standards.
- **Political/Regulatory Pragmatism**: Adopted a more pragmatic external posture (including high-profile appearances and engagement with the incoming administration) to reduce regulatory friction around domestic AI infrastructure buildout.
