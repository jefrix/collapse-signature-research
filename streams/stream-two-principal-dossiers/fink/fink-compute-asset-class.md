Strategic Framework: Compute as Infrastructure
Fink is positioning BlackRock at the center of a massive physical buildout, arguing that the AI transition requires a fundamental shift in capital markets and the state-capital relationship.

Financializing the Intelligence Age (May 5, 2026)
The Compute Asset Class: Speaking at the Milken Institute Global Conference, Fink stated, "I actually believe a new asset class will be buying futures of compute".

Resource Shortage: Fink warned of a systemic supply crunch: "We’re short power, we’re short compute, we’re short chips, and there are going to be shortages in all three, and memory".

The $10 Trillion Requirement: Onstage with Fink, Brookfield CEO Bruce Flatt estimated a $10 trillion requirement for power, AI factories, data centers and fiber build-out to rewire the world for the new economy. Fink noted that governments alone would not be able to fund the infrastructure expansion due to rising deficits, necessitating total reliance on private capital.

K-Shaped Stability Condition: Fink has previously argued that without broadened ownership—ensuring the middle class is invested in AI capital—the wealth gap will lead to rapid systemic failure (collapse).
