## 1. STATEMENT OF THE THREAD

The project's central question asks whether complex systems on the verge of collapse exhibit detectable signatures. The self-narrative of the system—whether it understands itself as rising or declining—may function as one such signature, with predictive power independent of the structural pressure field.

**Provisional hypothesis:** A system's self-narrative orientation (rising vs. declining) is not merely an epiphenomenon of structural conditions but an independent variable that shapes system outcomes, operating through elite motivation, institutional morale, and the imaginability of future action.

**Origin:** This thread emerged from the Late Tang / Ming consolidation comparative analysis, where the Tang post-755 literary corpus is saturated with decline-consciousness while the early Ming corpus is organized around restoration and renewal. The structural stress bundles are comparable (military-fiscal crisis, elite turnover, territorial contraction), but the self-narrative orientations are opposite, and so are the outcomes (simplification-survival vs. re-complexification).

## 2. CODING PROTOCOL

For each case in the dataset, code self-narrative on a five-point ordinal scale:

| Code | Label | Operational definition | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| +2 | Unambiguously rising | Texts frame the present as ascendant, the future as expansive; foundational stories are being actively claimed | Early Ming, early Roman Republic, post-1945 United States |
| +1 | Cautiously rising | Texts acknowledge difficulty but frame it as transitional; renewal is the dominant register | Post-1978 China, post-1945 Western Europe |
| 0 | Ambivalent | Rising and declining registers compete; no clear dominance | Late Roman Republic (optimates vs. populares), interwar Britain |
| -1 | Cautiously declining | Elegiac register dominates; renewal scripts are present but marginal | Late Habsburg (Roth, Zweig after 1918) |
| -2 | Unambiguously declining | The past is an organizing memory of lost greatness; the present is diminishment; the future is imaginable only as continuation of decline | Late Tang post-755, Western Roman 5th century, late Soviet (1980s) |

**Coding rule:** Code from the literary-strategic corpus (Stream Three), not from modern historians' assessments. The variable measures what the system's participants believed, not what later analysts conclude.

---

## 3. TESTABLE PREDICTIONS

1. **Cases with -2 self-narrative and high structural stress are more likely to collapse** than cases with +2 self-narrative and comparable structural stress.
2. **Cases with -2 self-narrative and moderate structural stress may collapse** at rates structural variables alone do not predict.
3. **Self-narrative direction change** (from rising to declining or vice versa) should correlate with structural inflection points, with the narrative shift potentially functioning as a leading indicator.

These predictions will be tested across the full dataset and stated as falsifiable claims in the book, timestamped per the project's methodological commitments.

## 4. INTEGRATION WITH OTHER STREAMS

- **Stream Three:** All historical cases coded for self-narrative using the literary-strategic module.

Monitoring for shifts in this orientation is a Stream Two task.
